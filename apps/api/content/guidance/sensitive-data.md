*This is a plain-language restatement of AI@UNC's acceptable-use guidance.
The policy itself is the authority — when in doubt, ask before you paste.*

## Generally fine (in approved tools)

- Public information: published budgets, posted policies, public web content
- Your own drafts and notes that contain no one else's personal information
- De-identified, aggregated numbers (totals by department, not by person)

## Be careful — approved tools only, and think first

- Internal working documents (unreleased budgets, draft org changes)
- Vendor quotes and non-public procurement details

Approved tools like [Microsoft 365 Copilot](/t/microsoft-365-copilot) keep
data inside UNC's tenant. A free public chatbot does not — the same document
that is fine in Copilot may be a policy violation in a consumer tool.

## Never, in any AI tool

- Student education records (FERPA)
- Social Security numbers, bank or payment card details
- Personnel records: salaries tied to names, performance reviews, health info
- Passwords, keys, or anything covered by a confidentiality agreement

## The 10-second test

Before pasting, ask: *"Would I email this to someone outside the
university?"* If no, check the tool's status in the registry first — and if
the tool isn't listed there at all, don't use it for work data.
