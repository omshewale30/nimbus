## What you'll do

Work through a backlog of email threads in a fraction of the usual time by
summarizing instead of reading, and drafting instead of composing.

## Prerequisites

- A Microsoft 365 Copilot license (the Copilot icon appears in the Outlook
  ribbon or above the reading pane).
- Works in new Outlook and Outlook on the web; classic Outlook support
  varies by version.

## Steps

1. Open a long thread and click **Summary by Copilot** at the top of the
   reading pane. You get the key points and who asked for what.
2. For threads that need a reply, click **Draft with Copilot** and give it
   one sentence of direction:
   > Reply confirming we'll have the reconciliation done by Friday, and ask
   > whether they need the detail by fund or just the total.
3. Adjust tone and length with the built-in options rather than re-prompting
   from scratch.
4. For threads where you may have made commitments, ask:
   > List anything in this thread that I promised to do, with dates.
5. **Read every draft fully before sending.** You are the sender; Copilot is
   not accountable for what goes out under your name.

## Common problems

- **Summary misses an attachment's content** — Copilot summarizes the
  thread text, not attachments. Open attachments separately.
- **Draft is too formal/stiff** — use the tone selector, or add "match the
  casual tone of the thread" to your direction.
- **Commitment list is incomplete** — treat it as a first pass, not an
  audit; it catches most but not all.
